How to Play Freebet Blackjack

The Basics

Freebet Blackjack plays like standard 6-deck blackjack — get closer to 21 than the dealer without busting, blackjack pays 3 to 2 — with a few house rules that favor the player less than they first appear: the dealer hits soft 17 (rather than standing), and there's no insurance offered (the dealer still peeks for blackjack on a ten or ace up-card, you just aren't offered a side wager on it).

The trade-off is a rule that only helps you: if the dealer busts with exactly 22, every hand still standing at 21 or less pushes instead of winning — except a player natural blackjack, which still wins 3 to 2 regardless.

Free Double

Any two-card hard total of 9, 10, or 11 (no ace) gets a free double automatically — the Double button relabels itself "Free Double" when you're eligible. Your original bet is matched with a free chip instead of your own money: lose the hand and you only lose your original bet, push and you get your original bet back, win and you're paid as if you'd doubled for real. Any other two-card total can still be doubled, but it costs real money like a normal double.

Free Split

Any pair except a pair of 10-value cards can be split for free — the new hand is staked with a free chip rather than your own money. Lose or push that hand and the free chip is simply taken back with no cost to you; win it and the free chip pays out like real money. Splitting a 10-value pair still works, it just costs you the normal matching bet. You can resplit up to 4 hands total (including aces), and double after split is always allowed.

Pot of Gold Side Bet

Every free double or free split you're dealt on a hand earns that hand one token — split for free then free-double the result and that's 2 tokens, for example. Pot of Gold is an optional side bet, placed once per hand before the deal, that pays out based on how many tokens that hand ends up with by the time it's done being played.

How to Play Super 4 Poker

The Basics

Super 4 Poker (also known as Crazy 4 Poker) is played with a single 52-card deck. You and the dealer each get 5 cards, and you both play your best 4-card hand — one card is always discarded. We automatically keep your best possible 4-card combination for you, so there's no discard decision to make; the dimmed card in your hand is the one that got cut.

Before the deal, you place equal Ante and X-Tra Bonus bets, plus an optional Player Bonus side bet. After seeing your cards, you either Fold (forfeiting everything) or make a Play wager to keep going — 1× your Ante normally, or up to 3× if you hold a pair of Aces or better.

4-Card Hand Rankings

With only 4 cards, the rankings work a little differently than regular poker — three of a kind is worth more than a flush here, for instance, since it's rarer with just 4 cards. Highest to lowest:

Four of a Kind → Straight Flush → Three of a Kind → Flush → Straight → Two Pair → Pair → High Card

Ante & Play

The dealer needs at least King-high to "open." If the dealer doesn't open, your Ante pushes and your Play bet wins automatically. If the dealer opens, your better 4-card hand wins both bets, a tie pushes both, and the dealer's better hand wins both.

X-Tra Bonus

Paid on your hand alone — the dealer's hand doesn't matter. A straight or better always wins the X-Tra Bonus at the table below. Anything weaker pushes if your Ante/Play won or pushed, and loses if your Ante/Play lost. Folding forfeits it.

We'll show a suggested action next to your hand, but the simple version: always take the 3× raise with a pair of Aces or better. With any other pair, take the 1× raise. How to Play Baccarat

The Basics

Baccarat is dealt from an 8-deck shoe, reshuffled fresh every round. Aces count as 1, 2 through 9 count as pip value, and 10s and face cards count as 0. Each hand's total is the sum of its cards' points, keeping only the last digit — a 9 and a 7 total 16, which counts as 6.

Player and Banker each start with 2 cards. If either has an 8 or 9 (a "natural"), both hands stand and the higher total wins. Otherwise, the Player draws a third card on 0-5 and stands on 6-7. The Banker's draw is more involved — it factors in the Player's third card when there is one — but it's all handled automatically according to the standard rules.

Whichever hand is closer to 9 wins. A tie pushes the Player and Banker bets.

Main Bets

Player pays 1 to 1. Banker pays 19 to 20 (even money less a 5% commission — the Banker has a positional edge, seeing the Player's decision before it acts). Tie pays 8 to 1 and simply loses if there's no tie — it does not push.

Pair Bets

Player Pair and Banker Pair each pay 10 to 1 if that hand's first two cards share the same rank.

5 Treasures Side Bets

Fortune 7 and Golden 8 need that hand to actually win. Heavenly 9 and Blazing 7s don't — they only care about how the cards landed.

How to Play Mines

The Basics

25 tiles, hidden mines — you choose how many (1 to 24). Place your bet, then reveal tiles one at a time. Every safe tile you find raises your multiplier. Hit a mine and the round ends with nothing; cash out anytime and you keep whatever your current multiplier is worth.

More mines means a bigger multiplier per safe tile, but a much higher chance of hitting one early. Fewer mines means slower, steadier growth.

The Math

Every multiplier is calculated directly from the odds of surviving that many reveals with your chosen mine count, then reduced by exactly 3%. That 3% is the entire house edge — it doesn't matter whether you cash out after 1 tile or 20, the expected return is always 97% of your bet. There's no hidden extra edge baked into risky play or safe play; the math is identical either way.

Multiplier after k safe reveals with m mines = [C(25,k) / C(25−m,k)] × 0.97, where C is "choose."
